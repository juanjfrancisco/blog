How to enable always-on feature for free on Azures App Service?

Do you find useful the free app services offered by azure?

Well, I do, it is a very good choice when it comes to test your application, but one thing that can be a little annoying is that Microsoft won’t let you use the feature for always-on, this one is only for basic or higher app service plan, but don’t worry I found a solution that can help us to accomplish the same behavior and completely for free!

The service name is called uptimerobot, a very simple to use service, let me show what we need to do:

  1. Register to create a free account

  2. After filling up all the information you will receive the activation info to your email account, go on with the boilerplate activation and login process, and once you are in the dashboard let’s configure our first monitor:

    After clicking “Add new monitor” our screen shows up and we choose monitor type https

    At this point we need to indicate the interval, you can use the default that is 5 minutes, normally the inactivity time for an azure app service before goes idle is 20 minutes, that’s way I use half (10 minutes). After that we are finished.

Now we can have our application always-on and we don’t have to worry anymore about the slow time of come back up every time our app goes idle. So go and give it a try!
